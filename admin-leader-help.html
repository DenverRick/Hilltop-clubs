This is where club leaders keep their own page on hilltopclubs.org up to date — your meeting schedule, description, photos, announcements, and more. Here's how it works.

Signing in

On any of the editing tabs, pick your club and enter the email address management has on file for you, then click Log in. That email is how we confirm it's really you — you can only edit your own club.

  • One sign-in covers all the tabs. Once you're in, switch between Meetings, About, and Outreach without logging in again.
  • Your browser remembers your sign-in so you don't retype it next time. On a shared computer, use "Forget saved sign-ins on this device."
  • If your email doesn't match, or you're not sure which one is on file, email Rick (below) and he'll sort it out.

The tabs

📅 Meetings & calendar

Your meeting schedule — this is what feeds the public club calendar and the "upcoming meetings" list on your club page.

  • Set your usual day, time, and location.
  • Under Your meetings & events, add a recurring meeting (it repeats on its own — weekly, every other week, or "the 1st Monday of the month," etc.). The form starts pre-filled from your saved meeting info, so you usually just confirm it.
  • Add a one-off event for a special occasion on a single date.
  • Use "Cancel a date" to skip a single week without deleting the whole meeting.
  • Prefer not to show specific dates? Check "Hide my upcoming meeting dates" — your meeting cadence still shows, just not the dated list.

📝 About the club

Everything that describes your club: a one-line blurb, a longer description, the vibe, what to bring for first-timers, tags, member count, your website / YouTube / TeamReach, and a banner photo and promo flyer.

📣 Announcements & outreach

  • Announcement — a timely banner on your card and page (e.g. a one-time event). Set an expiry date and it disappears on its own.
  • Draft a promo email — Claude AI writes a promo email from your club's info and next event; review it, then open it in your mail app.
  • RSVP — optionally collect attendance with a one-tap RSVP link instead of email replies.

Good to know

  • Leaving a field blank keeps the existing value — it doesn't erase it. To actually clear something, email Rick.
  • Your email is private — it's never shown on your public page. Residents reach you through an "I'm interested" button that opens a message in their own mail app.
  • Changes go live within about a minute.
